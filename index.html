What Is RELAY

One platform. Every operational layer.

RELAY is a fully custom-built internal workforce management and operational platform — designed, architected, and developed from the ground up. It replaces the fragmented mix of spreadsheets, email threads, and disconnected tools that most organizations rely on, bringing every core operational function into a single, unified system.

From day-to-day team management to long-term professional development tracking, RELAY gives leadership complete visibility and control — and gives employees a single place to operate from. Built for scale, built for real organizations, and built to connect with the systems already in use.

Platform Modules

Everything an organization actually needs.

Team Management Hub

Centralized team directory, role management, org structure, and personnel data — with admin controls and manager-level views built separately through role-based access.

Professional Development Tracking

Individual development plans, goal tracking, training completions, and milestone logging — giving both employees and leadership a real-time view of growth and progress over time.

Scheduling & Availability

Team scheduling, shift management, and availability tracking — built into the platform so managers aren't chasing information across three different tools.

Task & Project Management

Assignable tasks, team project tracking, and accountability systems — so nothing falls through the cracks and leadership can see what's moving and what's stalled.

Announcements & Communication

Internal announcements, team-wide communications, and meeting notes — centralized so information reaches the right people through the right channels.

Admin & Manager Dashboards

Separate dashboard views for administrators and managers — role-based access ensures the right people see the right data, with audit controls built in at every level.

Technical Architecture

Built to scale. Built to integrate.

RELAY was built with the infrastructure to grow beyond its initial deployment. The architecture supports full integration with third-party HR and workforce management systems — meaning organizations don't have to abandon their existing tech stack to adopt it. RELAY connects, extends, and enhances what's already in place.

The front end and back end were built and managed entirely by one person. Real-time database sync via Supabase ensures every update is reflected instantly across all users and roles, with row-level security policies protecting data integrity across the platform.

The Recognized Need

Accountability has a platform problem.

Social media was built for attention, not intention. The tools people were using to try to stay accountable — posting to feeds, using notes apps, texting friends — weren't built for that purpose. They were borrowed solutions for a real problem that nobody had built a dedicated answer to.

The need was clear: a platform designed from the ground up around accountability — where communities are built around shared goals, progress is visible and encouraged, and the infrastructure supports both individual users and the businesses and organizations trying to reach them.

PROOOF Social was conceived as that platform. Not another social app with accountability features bolted on — a purpose-built ecosystem where holding yourself and each other to a standard is the entire point.

The Solution

Built from zero. Every layer.

As founder and sole developer, I designed and built the entire platform — product architecture, UI/UX, backend infrastructure, data modeling, and admin tooling. No co-founder. No development team. Every decision, from database schema to screen layout, was owned and executed end to end.

Social Feeds & Profiles

User authentication, personal profiles, activity feeds, and social discovery built for engagement and community-first interaction.

School & Community Pages

Dedicated community hubs for universities including OU, OSU, UCO, and SWOSU — with location-based discovery and member engagement.

Business Profiles & Dashboards

Owner dashboards for business accounts with analytics, post management, and tools designed for brand presence within the platform.

Plan-Based Feature Gating

Free, Pro, and Team tiers with distinct feature sets — built with scalable logic so monetization gates expand as the platform grows.

Web Admin Dashboard

A full administrative control panel with service-role Supabase access — user management, content moderation, and platform analytics.

Discover Screen

How It Started

It began with the data.

The engagement with RoadRunner Scooters started with a marketing data analysis — a deep dive into what was working, what wasn't, and where the brand's digital presence had gaps. What I found was a company with strong products and a compelling story that wasn't translating effectively online.

The content wasn't consistent. The SEO foundation was thin. Social channels were underutilized. And there was no internal system keeping any of it organized or moving forward. That analysis became the starting point for an ongoing engagement that grew well beyond the initial scope.

Scope of Work

From analysis to full management.

What started as an audit evolved into end-to-end content and marketing management. Over time the engagement expanded to cover every major touchpoint of RoadRunner's digital presence.

SEO Strategy & Blog Management

Developed and managed an SEO-driven content calendar — researching keywords, writing and publishing blog content, and building internal linking structures to grow organic search visibility over time.

Social Platform Strategy

Built out multi-platform social strategy — content pillars, posting cadence, caption writing, and creative direction aligned to the RoadRunner brand voice and target audience across all major channels.

Email Campaigns

Wrote and managed email marketing campaigns — product launches, promotions, and brand storytelling — with subject line strategy, copy, and performance tracking built in.

Internal SOPs

Developed internal standard operating procedures for content workflows — ensuring consistency, quality control, and a clear process for how content moved from brief to publish across all channels.

Marketing Data Analysis

Ongoing analysis of content performance, engagement trends, and channel health — using data to inform what gets created, when it goes out, and how the strategy evolves over time.

In-Person HQ Support

Traveled to RoadRunner's headquarters in Denver, CO to provide on-site strategic support — working directly with the team to align content operations, creative direction, and brand execution at the source.

Most content engagements are fully remote and transactional. This one wasn't. The in-person presence at the Denver HQ meant I could understand the product, the team, and the brand culture firsthand — and that understanding shows in the content.

RoadRunner isn't just a client — it's a brand I know. The scooters, the lineup, the differentiators (IP67 water protection, US-owned, Denver-built identity) — I know them because I spent time in the room where the brand lives, not just working from a brief.

The Backstory

Two closures. One opportunity.

When two major corporate fitness franchises closed their Oklahoma City locations, it created both a gap in the market and an opportunity for something better. Two separate ownership groups chose to consolidate — merging their communities, their resources, and their vision into a single, privately-owned fitness brand that would be built to last.

The challenge: none of that vision had a name, a face, or a system behind it yet. That's where I came in. What they had was a concept, a community of members they wanted to keep, and a real chance to build something that didn't look or feel like a corporate franchise. What they needed was someone to turn that into a tangible, scalable brand.

What I Built

Vision to brand. Concept to community.

Club Fitness didn't exist before this engagement. The name, the visual identity, the voice, the digital presence — all of it was created from scratch.

Brand Identity Creation

Logo system, color palette, typography, and brand marks — built cohesively so every touchpoint looks like it belongs to the same brand.

Brand Voice & Positioning

Defined how Club Fitness communicates — community-driven and personal, without losing the polish expected of a serious fitness brand.

Community Retention Strategy

The merged brand inherited members from both closed franchises. Built the messaging and content strategy to retain that existing community through the transition.

The Result

A brand OKC actually knows.

Club Fitness is now a recognized name in the Oklahoma City fitness community. From two closed corporate franchises with no shared identity, the brand launched, built its audience, and grew to 500+ active members — with a visual identity and brand system that makes it look and feel like it was always here.

That's what brand strategy is supposed to do. Not just look good on a launch post — build something that earns recognition over time and gives a business the foundation it needs to keep growing.
